Exploration 3: 12 Angry Men

I was looking for an older movie to watch because the rest of mine were in theaters recently, and I settled upon 12 Angry Men (1h 36m). For those who don’t know, 12 Angry Men is about a jury deciding whether to convict a teenager of killing his father, needing a unanimous vote either way. At the beginning, 11 vote guilty, but juror #8 (Henry Fonda) votes not guilty, wishing to re-examine the case. He aims to provide a “reasonable doubt” as to whether the boy is guilty, and the rest of the movie is him picking apart the case of the prosecution. I really enjoyed the logic that was presented on both sides and following the men of the jury as they wonder whether the boy is definitely guilty.
